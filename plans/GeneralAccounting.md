# 1. Introduction

This document explains how accounting works for a **Ukrainian sole proprietor (FOP)** with ~3 employees, including:

- Tax systems
- Taxes and payments
- Income recording (what it *actually* means)
- Cash vs non-cash operations
- Documents and digital workflows
- Reporting (“Zvit”)
- Counteragents (контрагенти)
- Real-life workflows

This is a **practical guide**, not theoretical law.

---

# 2. Tax Systems in Ukraine

## 2.1 Simplified System (Most Common)

### Group 2
- Fixed monthly tax
- Up to 10 employees
- Mostly local services

### Group 3
- 5% of income (+ ~1% military levy)
- Unlimited employees
- Works well for IT, consulting, exports

👉 Most flexible and commonly used for modern businesses

---

## 2.2 General System

- 18% income tax
- 5% military levy
- 22% social contribution (ESV)
- Based on **profit (income – expenses)**

👉 Used when:
- Expenses are high
- Income exceeds simplified limits

---

# 3. Taxes

## 3.1 FOP Taxes (Group 3 example)

- Single Tax: ~5% of income
- Military Levy: ~1%
- ESV (ЄСВ): ~22% of minimum salary (fixed minimum)

---

## 3.2 Payment Schedule

- Taxes → quarterly
- ESV → quarterly
- Deadlines strict → penalties if missed

---

# 4. Employees (Critical Area)

## 4.1 Taxes on Employees

### Withheld:
- 18% Personal Income Tax
- 5% Military Levy

### Paid by employer:
- 22% ESV

---

## 4.2 Responsibilities

You act as a **tax agent**, meaning:
- You calculate taxes
- You withhold taxes
- You pay taxes on behalf of employees
- You report them

---

# 5. What “Record Income” Really Means

## 5.1 Core Principle

> Income is recorded when **money is received** (cash basis)

---

## 5.2 Minimum Requirement

Maintain a structured income register:

| Date | Amount | Counteragent | Basis |
|------|--------|--------------|--------|
| 01 Mar | 1000 USD | ABC Ltd | Invoice #12 |

---

## 5.3 Is Bank Export Enough?

### Yes (partially):
- Dates ✔
- Amounts ✔
- Counterparties ✔

### No (fully):
- No business meaning
- No document linkage

---

## 5.4 Best Practice

Combine:
- Bank statement
- Simple structured log

Tool examples:
- Excel / Google Sheets
- Accounting software

---

# 6. Cash vs Non-Cash

## 6.1 Non-Cash (Recommended)

- Bank transfers only
- Simplest compliance
- No cash register needed

---

## 6.2 Cash

If you accept cash:
- Must record it
- Must issue receipt

---

## 6.3 Cash Registers (RRO / PRRO)

Required if:
- You sell to individuals (B2C)
- You accept cash or card payments

---

## 6.4 Simplest Setup

> No cash + only bank transfers = minimal complexity

# 9. Zvit (Tax Reporting)

## 9.1 What is Zvit?

Tax declaration submitted to authorities.

---

## 9.2 How to Submit

### Option 1: Tax Cabinet
- Log in
- Fill form
- Sign (QES)
- Submit

---

### Option 2: Software
- Auto-calculation
- Easier UI

---

### Option 3: Accountant
- Fully outsourced

---

## 9.3 Process

1. Calculate income
2. Fill form
3. Validate
4. Sign
5. Submit
6. Receive confirmations

---

## 9.4 Deadlines

- Submit → within 40 days after quarter
- Pay → within 10 days after

---

## 9.5 Critical Detail

Must receive:
- Receipt #1 (received)
- Receipt #2 (accepted)

---

# 10. Counteragents (Контрагенти)

## 10.1 Definition

A counteragent is any party you transact with:
- Clients
- Suppliers
- Contractors

---

## 10.2 Why Important

They provide:
- Proof of income origin
- Business legitimacy
- Audit traceability

---

## 10.3 Required Data

For each counteragent:

- Name
- Tax ID (EDRPOU / INN)
- Country
- Contract reference
- Payment records

---

## 10.4 Required Documents

### Standard Set

1. Contract
2. Invoice
3. Act of completed work

---

## 10.5 Foreign Counteragents

Typical setup:

- Contract (often English)
- Invoice
- Bank confirmation

Important:
- Income recorded in UAH equivalent
- Bank handles currency control

---

## 10.6 Risks

Red flags:

- Missing contracts
- Unexplained payments
- Fake counterparties
- Document mismatch

---

## 10.7 Best Practices

- Link every payment to:
    - Contract
    - Invoice
- Store per counteragent
- Use consistent naming

Example:
/Clients/
/ABC_Ltd/
Contract.pdf
Invoice_12.pdf
Act_12.pdf


---

# 11. Practical Workflow

## Monthly

1. Receive income → record it
2. Pay salaries
3. Withhold taxes
4. Pay payroll taxes
5. Track totals

---

## Quarterly

1. Sum income
2. Submit Zvit
3. Pay taxes

---

# 12. Clean Setup (Recommended)

- Separate business account
- No cash usage
- Digital-only documents
- Simple income log
- Electronic reporting

---

# 13. Common Mistakes

- Missing deadlines
- Not paying ESV with zero income
- Mixing personal/business money
- Poor documentation
- Ignoring counteragents

---

# 14. Key Takeaways

Ukrainian FOP accounting is not traditional accounting.

It is:

> A structured system of:
> - Tracking income
> - Managing documents
> - Handling taxes
> - Reporting regularly

---

## Final Insight

The system becomes simple if you:

- Avoid cash
- Keep documents organized
- Link all payments to real business activity
- Submit reports on time

Otherwise, it becomes risky very quickly.
